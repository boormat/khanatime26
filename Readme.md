
Project Goals:

Develop application to use for Rally and Khanacross result collection, merging and publishing event results.

This is possibly 2 applications, but would be good to share a common deployment communications mechanism for simplicity.

These events are low budget motorsport events.  Officials are all unpaid volunteers.
Keeping costs and hassle to a minimum is important.

Being able to let officials get connected with the app easily is important as they will often forget to download an app until at the event.  This means downloads must be small to download in a reasonable time at slow data rate, or able to be loaded from a local server or other device.

Officials are often reluctant to install apps and mess with phone settings, or simply not familiar with it, so must be simple as possible.

## Khanacross 

Khanas are held in a small area, typically a paddock or similar ground.  The competitive timed parts are called tests.  Depending on the event, the competitor can have 1 or 2 or more attempts at the test to get their best time.  Tests are typically under 1km, so times are around 1 minute.  Tests often start and stop at the same spot, so an official can time with a stopwatch.  Typically a few officials time for redundancy as stopwatches are easy to mess up.  Officials also record penalties, such as hitting flags or not stopping inside the stop area, or going the wrong direction on the course.   Some khanas have separate start and stop areas, so a stopwatch cannot be used.  These operate by the relevant officials recording the start and finish times separately, and then the results being forwarded to the Timekeeper who calculates the time taked.  Start times are typically on the turn of the minute for convenience.   The Timer is at event HQ/service/parking area.  The events might have phone coverage.  The event area is small enough a isolated Wifi net could be setup where there is no phone coverage to allow for communications.
Timekeeper is typically at HQ, and is responsible for gathering the results for each test, and generating the results.  Timekeepers finalise the results making adjeustments where required, e.g. where more than one stopwatch result or other timer, and sort out data entry mistakes, and final say on penalties and other adjustments.  The event has a number of tests typically 5 to 10.  The number of entrants typically 20 to 50.
Competitors are in the HQ area, and should be able to use the same app to see the results.  Competitors should see only the approved results from the timekeeper, or at least indicate when the results are finalised.


Having a voice broadcast capability would be helpfull to remove the need for walkie talkies.  Chat messaging with good text to voice and voice to text might be good enough.

## Rallys and RallySprints

Rallies and Rallysprints cover a much larger area and typically held in forest roads. A Rally stage is typically 10 to 50km, a Rallysprint stage 3 to 10kms.  Both are going to start and finish in separate areas, so timing is done by officials recording start and finish times by wall clock time.  GPS time sources are often used, or synchronised clocks.  Some events use beams to get sub second accuracy of finish times, and to detect competitors jumping the start.   Stages use a allocated start time, typically on the turn of the minute.  Stages keep a minimum time between each competitor at the start, typically 2 minutes. At stage finishes competitors could be out of order, either due to overtaking, or a competitor stoppoing in stage due to a crash or failure.
Additional officials may be part way through the stage for safety monitoring.  These officials log the cars as they pass, recording the number and time.  This is important to help locate cars that might crash off the road in stage and be not visible.  They also serve as a communication point to relay any accident reports to HQ, that might be relayed by a competitor. 

Phone coverage for stage officials and competitors is often very intermittent.  Some start and stop points will not have phone coverage.  Radio voice coverage is often poor quality or unreliable.  The use of a reliable app mechanism to forward competitor tracking and times to HQ is highly desirable.  Competitors will move in and out of mobile reception, so being able to automatically store and forward tracking and results from an official to the competitors and then forward to HQ when phone reception allows is a important feature.   It should also store and forward messages between Competitors  to get the most reliable and timely delivery possible.  Competitors are at the start and finish points for a few minutes, within 5 meters of the officials, which should be fine for bluetooth and/or a Wifi hotspot or router.
For simplicity, we should only use mobile phones, and not require start/stop officials or competitors to setup extra equipment such as a wifi router.  Keeping battery use low is desirable.
